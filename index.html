üîÑ Implementation Process in Six Stages

1
Submit Trial Request
The project submits a request to enter the sandbox with innovation field definition and risk management
2
Committee Discussion
The committee reviews the request and approves entry into the experimental sandbox
3
RFI, Development & Trial
Publishing RFI, controlled experimentation in supervised environment, and quarterly progress reports
4
Submit System-Wide Implementation Request
Upon trial completion - submitting models and recommendations for systemic implementation
5
Committee Discussion
The committee reviews results and approves transition to system-wide implementation
6
System-Wide Implementation
Scaling and embedding the innovative model throughout the entire education system
üîç Three Key Insights
1. Examine Existing Solutions in the System: At times, solutions to innovation challenges are already present within the system - there's no need for new legislation, it's enough to rediscover and activate existing tools.

2. Protected and Bounded Space: It's recommended to work in a limited scope and time-bound framework - controlled experimentation that enables learning and risk management before scaling to the entire system.

3. Engage Partners from the Start: It's important to ensure connection and updates with relevant stakeholders. Engaging relevant regulators from the beginning can remove barriers down the road.
üí° Core Principle: RRI - Responsible Research and Innovation
A process that takes into account the actual and potential impact on the environment and society during research and development, from ethical, legal, and social aspects. Every project must demonstrate compliance with responsible innovation principles.
